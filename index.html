Rajvi Agravat
Auditory Neuroscientist

Hello! I am a Ph.D. student in Neuroscience at The University of Texas at Austin, specializing in speech, auditory, and attention neuroscience. I investigate the neural mechanisms of speech perception, particularly in naturalistic 'cocktail party' scenarios, using direct brain recordings (stero EEG) and computational modeling. By investigating implicit (stimulus-driven) and explicit (goal-directed) attention, I aim to inform the design of hearing technologies, AI-driven speech systems (STT/TTS), and therapeutic interventions for cognitive-linguistic disorders. With over 6 years of experience in neuroscience research, I have worked on diverse projects. My work integrates advanced techniques such as acoustic modeling, source separation deep neural networks, and encoding models to predict brain activity in response to complex auditory stimuli. I also develop game-based paradigms to study cognitive-load and attention dynamics. Beyond the lab, I am passionate about open science, mentorship, and bridging gaps between neuroscience, engineering, and clinical applications.

I am also passionate about UX Research and harnessing research findings to design inclusive technology that improves the way people interact with the world around them.

Promoting diversity and mentorship in STEM is deeply important to me. As a woman in STEM, I am interested in empowering the next generation of scientists through mentorship and professional development opportunities.

I have presented my research at conferences like Advances and Perspectives in Auditory Neuroscience (APAN), Society for Neuroscience (SfN) and received research awards from organizations like the Texas SLH Foundation.

Outside of work, you will find me trying new restaurants and cuisines, reading at a coffee shop, volunteering at the Austin Animal Shelter, or planning my next travel. :)
