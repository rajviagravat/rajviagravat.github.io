Rajvi Agravat

Hello! I am a Ph.D. student in Neuroscience at The University of Texas at Austin. In the Hamilton Lab, I study auditory attention in healthy individuals and individuals with epilepsy, using electrophysiology techniques like electroencephalography (EEG) and intracranial EEG (sEEG). This project examines selective auditory attention and speech in noise using a multidisciplinary approach, combining game-based training and EEG to understand neural processes in complex acoustic environments. Concurrently, for another project, I use speech and music encoding models to understand how different brain areas represent acoustic features of speech and music, such as spectrograms and pitch.

Before moving to Austin for my Ph.D. in August 2022, I was a Research Assitant in Erich Jarvis' Lab at The Rockefeller University, New York. Here I conducted retrograde and anterograde viral tracing experiments to map out the connectivity between the laryngeal motor cortex and orofacial motor cortex. Both regions are associated with vocal communication in mice.

With over 5 years of experience in neuroscience research, I have worked on diverse projects, such as neural connectivity mapping in vocal learning circuits, executive function task processing in children, and carrying out neuropsychological assessments for individuals with stroke and epilepsy.

Promoting diversity and mentorship in STEM is deeply important to me. As a woman in STEM, I am interested in empowering the next generation of scientists through mentorship and professional development opportunities.

I have presented my research at conferences like Society for Neuroscience (SfN) and received awards like the Texas SLH Foundation Research Award. Beyond research, I volunteer for causes like animal welfare and children's education.

Outside of work, you will find me trying new restaurants and cuisines, reading at a coffee shop, volunteering at the Austin Animal Shelter, or planning my next travel. :)
